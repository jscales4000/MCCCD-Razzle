Touch Target Sizing — Home Buttons

Compare actual rendered sizes
WCAG 2.5.5 — minimum touch target = 44 × 44 CSS px. Anything below that fails AA.
01 Header Nav Cameras / Audio / Settings
Current ~30 × 95 Fails AA
Medium 40 × 120 Borderline
Large 52 × 150 Passes AA
Trade-off: Larger targets eat header height — current header is ~56px tall, so 52px nav buttons demand a 70px+ header. Recommendation: Medium (40×120) is the comfort sweet spot for stylus & finger; Large for ADA-strict deployments.
02 Advanced Routing Must POP — never dark
Compact Accent 40 × 140 Borderline
Prominent 52 × 180 Passes AA
Hero Placement 60 × 220 Passes AA
Trade-off: Compact reads as a chip in the source row; Prominent reads as a CTA below the row; Hero claims dedicated real-estate as a deliberate primary action. Recommendation: Prominent (52×180) — it pops, hits AA, and doesn't dominate the page.
03 Mic Toggles Lav / Handheld
Current 30 × 80 Fails AA
Medium 44 × 100 Passes AA
Large 56 × 120 Passes AA
Trade-off: Larger mic toggles let us surface live status inline (no second pill needed). Recommendation: Medium (44×100) hits the WCAG floor exactly; Large is worth it if we move "Live" status into the button itself.
04 Volume Vol− / Mute / Vol+
Current 30 × 60 Fails AA
Medium 44 × 80 Passes AA
Large 60 × 100 Passes AA
Trade-off: Volume is the most-tapped control — the current 30×60 is well below WCAG. All three variants stay transparent so the footer rhythm holds. Recommendation: Large (60×100) with text labels. The labels disambiguate icon-only ambiguity and the footprint still leaves room for mic + adv-routing.
